Common Winter Plumbing Problems in Buffalo Homes

Frozen Pipes

When temperatures drop below freezing, pipes in unheated spaces are at risk of freezing. This can lead to burst pipes, requiring emergency drain installation or extensive repairs. Areas most vulnerable include:

  • Basement plumbing
  • Pipes in exterior walls
  • Garden hose connections
  • Garage plumbing fixtures

Frozen Faucets

Outdoor faucets are particularly susceptible to freezing. If you're experiencing issues, our faucet repair services can help prevent costly damage.

Preventive Measures for Winter Plumbing Protection

As discussed in our Ultimate Guide to Residential Plumbing Services, prevention is key to avoiding winter plumbing disasters.

Essential Winter Preparation Steps

  • Insulate exposed pipes, especially in West Side homes with older construction
  • Keep your home heated to at least 55Â°F, even when away
  • Let faucets drip during extreme cold spells
  • Know the location of your main water shut-off valve

Signs of Winter Plumbing Problems

Watch for these warning signs that may require immediate attention:

  • Reduced water pressure
  • Strange noises from pipes
  • Visible frost on exposed pipes
  • Unusual odors from drains
